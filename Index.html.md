## 4. Campaign Outreach Strategy by Segment

### Segment A: SPIKE Accounts — "Capitalize on the Moment"

**Why they matter:** A large spike often means a new workload went live, a migration completed, or a product launch drove sudden demand. This is the best time to engage — they're actively scaling.

**Outreach approach:**
1. **Subject:** Your Azure momentum in [Spike Month] — let's build on it
2. **Hook:** Reference the specific growth signal — "I noticed your Azure usage jumped significantly in [month]"
3. **Value prop:** Offer cost optimization for the new workload (Reserved Instances, Savings Plans) + architecture review
4. **Ask:** 20-min call to discuss what drove the spike and how to optimize for it
5. **MACC angle:** If spike is sustained 2+ months, introduce commitment conversation with savings modeling

### Segment B: FAST GROWING Accounts — "Partner for the Long Run"

**Why they matter:** Sustained growth = the product-market fit is working and infrastructure demand will keep rising. These are your best long-term MACC candidates.

**Outreach approach:**
1. **Subject:** [Account Name]'s Azure trajectory — how we can help accelerate it
2. **Hook:** Reference multi-month growth pattern — "Over the past 9 months, your Azure consumption has grown X%"
3. **Value prop:** Strategic commitment discussion (MACC), architecture review for scale, specialist connections
4. **Ask:** Working session to model commitment scenarios and savings
5. **MACC angle:** Strong fit — project forward 12 months and show savings from a MACC at their run rate

### Segment C: NEW ACR Accounts — "Welcome and Expand"

**Why they matter:** They just adopted Azure. The first 3-6 months set the trajectory. Early engagement = higher retention and faster expansion.

**Outreach approach:**
1. **Subject:** Welcome to Azure — a few things to help [Account Name] get the most out of it
2. **Hook:** Acknowledge they're new — "I noticed [Account Name] recently started using Azure"
3. **Value prop:** Onboarding support, architecture guidance, Microsoft for Startups credits (if applicable), specialist introductions
4. **Ask:** Intro call to understand their roadmap and ensure they're set up for success
5. **MACC angle:** Too early for commitment — focus on expanding workloads and building trust first
