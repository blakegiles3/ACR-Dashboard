## 2. Campaign Structure — 3-Touch Sequence

### Touch 1: "Growth Recognition" Email (Day 1)
**Purpose:** Open the conversation by acknowledging their success and Azure growth.

**Subject Line Options (pick one):**
- Your Azure momentum — let's make it count
- Noticed something impressive about [Account Name]'s cloud trajectory
- [Account Name] + Azure: let's talk about what's next

**Email Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I've been reviewing [Account Name]'s Azure footprint and wanted to reach out because something stood out — your [Top Growing Service] usage has grown significantly over the past few months, and your overall Azure consumption is trending well above forecast.

That kind of growth usually signals that your engineering team is shipping fast and scaling infrastructure to match. That's great to see.

I'm reaching out because when we see this pattern with Digital Natives customers, there are usually a few things we can help with:

- **Cost optimization at scale** — making sure your architecture is efficient as you grow (reserved instances, spot VMs, right-sizing)
- **Technical deep-dives** — connecting you with Azure engineering specialists in [Top Growing Service] to unlock capabilities you might not be using yet
- **Commitment planning** — if your trajectory continues, a MACC commitment could unlock meaningful discounts and credits

Would you have 20 minutes this week or next for a quick call? I'd love to hear what's driving the growth and explore how we can support it.

Best,
[Your Name]

---

### Touch 2: "Value Add" Follow-Up (Day 5-7)
**Purpose:** Share a relevant insight or asset — position yourself as a helpful resource, not just a seller.

**Subject Line Options:**
- Quick insight on [Top Growing Service] at scale
- A few things other DN customers are doing with [Top Growing Service]
- Thought this might be useful for [Account Name]

**Email Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Following up on my note last week. I wanted to share something that might be relevant given your [Top Growing Service] growth:

**[Include ONE of the following based on the account's top growing service]**

**If Compute/VMs are growing:**
Several of our Digital Natives customers who saw similar VM scaling have saved 30-45% by combining Reserved Instances with Azure Spot VMs for their batch/dev workloads. Happy to connect you with our optimization team for a quick architecture review.

**If AI/ML services are growing:**
With your AI services usage accelerating, you might be interested in our Azure AI Foundry preview program — we're giving select DN customers early access to new model hosting capabilities and dedicated capacity. Want me to check eligibility?

**If Data/Analytics services are growing:**
I've seen a few companies at your scale make the shift from self-managed analytics to Microsoft Fabric with strong results (60-70% less pipeline maintenance). Given your data platform growth, this might be worth exploring.

**If Kubernetes/Containers are growing:**
Your AKS growth is impressive. We recently launched AKS Automatic which removes a lot of the cluster management overhead — several DN customers have cut their platform engineering time by 40%. Worth a look?

**If Networking/CDN is growing:**
With your networking footprint expanding, Azure Front Door's new origin-level caching and private link support could meaningfully improve performance and reduce egress costs. Happy to set up a brief technical session.

No pressure at all — just wanted to make sure you had this on your radar. Let me know if any of this is interesting and I'll set up the right conversation.

Best,
[Your Name]

## 3. Campaign Execution Playbook

### Week 1: Preparation
- [ ] Export top 15 accounts from ACR Anomaly Dashboard (filter: large increase anomalies, sort by anomaly magnitude)
- [ ] Fill in the Account Tracker table above with TPID, YTD ACR, anomaly details
- [ ] For each account, pull monthly trend and service breakdown to identify top growing service
- [ ] Check MACC status for commitment conversation readiness
- [ ] Personalize Touch 1 emails for all 15 accounts

### Week 2: Touch 1 Outreach
- [ ] Send Touch 1 emails (stagger: 5 per day over 3 days)
- [ ] Log all sends in Account Tracker
- [ ] Monitor for replies — prioritize responders for accelerated engagement

### Week 3: Touch 2 Follow-Up
- [ ] Send Touch 2 to non-responders (Day 5-7 from Touch 1)
- [ ] For responders: schedule calls and prepare account-specific decks
- [ ] Update Account Tracker with engagement status

### Week 4: Touch 3 Close
- [ ] Send Touch 3A to engaged accounts (with commitment proposal)
- [ ] Send Touch 3B to non-responders (graceful close, leave door open)
- [ ] Hand off qualified opportunities to account team / sales

### Ongoing: Post-Campaign
- [ ] Re-check ACR anomaly dashboard monthly for new surge accounts
- [ ] Rinse and repeat for next batch of 15
- [ ] Track conversion rate: outreach to meeting to opportunity to MACC close

## 5. Personalization Cheat Sheet

For each account, gather these data points before outreach:

- Account Name
- TPID
- FY26 YTD ACR
- Monthly trend (start month ACR to current month ACR, % growth, # months)
- Top growing service
- Anomaly size ($ and % above expected)
- MACC status (Active / Expiring / None)
- MACC balance remaining
- Key contact name and role
- Recent news or funding rounds

**Pro tip:** Use the sdp-acr tools (get_acr_monthly_trend, get_acr_by_service, get_macc_summary) in GitHub Copilot CLI to pull each account's data. Just provide the TPID and FY26.
